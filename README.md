# predictive-cart-abandonment-intelligence
### Behavior-Driven Analytics & Decision Support System
Conceptual analytics project designing a predictive cart abandonment intelligence system for Amazon. Combines behavioral segmentation, abandonment risk modeling, friction analysis, dashboards, and a scalable roadmap to support proactive, ethical intervention strategies.

---

## Project Overview

This project explores how Amazon could evolve from reactive cart recovery tactics to a **predictive, behavior-driven cart abandonment intelligence system**.

Rather than relying on broad discounts or post-abandonment emails, we design a framework that identifies:
- **Which customers are likely to abandon**
- **Why abandonment occurs**
- **When and how to intervene effectively**

> *This is a conceptual analytics capstone. No proprietary Amazon data or production systems were used.*

---

## Business Problem

High-intent shoppers frequently abandon carts at the final stage of checkout, resulting in significant lost revenue.  
However, not all abandonment represents lost demandâ€”some behavior reflects browsing, price comparison, or delayed decision-making.

**Core challenge:**  
Differentiate **harmful abandonment** from benign behavior *before checkout* and intervene selectively.

---

## Solution Concept

We propose a **cart abandonment intelligence framework** that combines behavioral analytics, predictive modeling, and executive dashboards to support proactive decision-making.

### Key Capabilities
- Predict abandonment risk in real time  
- Identify behavioral and friction drivers  
- Segment customers by intent and sensitivity  
- Recommend targeted interventions  
- Support scalable and ethical deployment  

---

## Data Strategy (Conceptual)

The solution is designed around first-party behavioral and transactional signals:

- Behavioral session logs  
- Cart activity events  
- Product attributes and price fluctuations  
- Shipping and delivery estimates  
- Review sentiment & friction signals (NLP)  
- Recommendation interaction signals  

All data considerations align with privacy and consent principles (GDPR, CCPA).

---

## Modeling Approach (Design-Level)

The proposed framework consists of four complementary components:

1. **Behavioral Segmentation**  
   Identify shopper profiles based on browsing and decision patterns  

2. **Cart Abandonment Prediction**  
   Estimate abandonment likelihood using behavioral and friction signals  

3. **Satisfaction & Friction Driver Analysis**  
   Use NLP and feature importance to surface root causes of hesitation  

4. **Intervention Uplift Modeling**  
   Match customer segments with the most effective intervention strategy  

> These models are proposed architectures rather than implemented systems.
